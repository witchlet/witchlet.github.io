Glasses

conclusion




i think that if i continue doing art that plays with perception, i should put more thought into critical theory and the art's historical/political/social context. because this was my first foray into this type of work, i treated it more like a study. that being said, i do think it was a successful study, and i do think that i created a fun little object. the main thing i learned from making this is the value of letting myself make a low-stakes, small-scale project largely from intuition. a lot of the work i do is large-scale and ambitious, even when it's my first time working in the medium. for instance, i recently made an inflatable for the first time, and it was 8 ft tall. i think if i did more quick practice projects before working towards the end goal of the inflatable, it would have been a much smoother process. i think the biggest fail was the conceptual aspect of the project, because, honestly, when i was making it, i was thinking about it more as a toy than an art object. i also could have better craftsmanship for future projects -- i was working very quick and dirty and scrappy for this.



i am happy with the objects that i made, and having made them, i know i can make more complex, interesting works in the future that has more of my artistic voice.
