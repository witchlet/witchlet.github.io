Glasses

development + technical implementation





this project is extremely simple, and was extremely simple to make. i didn't even sketch the design before making it. the decision that took the longest was deciding the color scheme. each variation consists of a ball bearing, two pieces of laser-cut acrylic, and two screws to keep the acrylic in place. i thought i would have to build my own ball bearings, which would have been the hard part, but i was told that the shop actually has ball bearings to use. so, all i had to was laser cut the acrylic and screw them in place. designing the shape to laser cut the acrylic into took, maybe, 2 minutes, so i didn't save the file. so i can't show it. i created a semicircle, and then boolean cut a semicircle the size of the ball bearing from the flat side of the semicircle. i prototyped with two cardboard pieces at first, and it worked fine, so i did the acrylic next. unfortunately, one of the pieces was ever-so-slightly too small, so there's a little nick at the edge of the orange acrylic. then, i marked and drilled holes into the acrylic, and screwed them in place.
