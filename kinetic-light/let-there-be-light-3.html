development + technical implementation


My original concept was to make a wireframe sculpture of two hands holding each other using lasers reflected by mirrors at specific angles to create the form, and it was going to be called "...so I don't lose you in the crowd". However, that would have defeinitely been too difficult, so I decided to make an altar-like structure of pillars with lasers reflected to make a ring of light at the top of the structure.




At first, the process was pretty easy, and I was making very good progress. I started off by cutting the pillars using the miter saw, then laser cut the bases for the pillars, as well as the mirrors. I hand-painted a mixture of acrylic paint and alcohol onto the bases, so that they would look darker, sort of like a wood stain. I then attached the pillars to the bases, and painted the pillars. It was easier to paint them when I knew they wouldn't fall over. Then, I moved everything to the third floor, and positioned the pillars into two overlapping circles. I had to redo the second circle two times, because I accidentally positioned the second circle's pillars in such a way that a pillar from both circles needed to be placed in the same position. That was the part I got stuck on the most. Working on the third floor was also pretty difficult, because I was doing precise measurements, and the lights were off. Then, I tested out the lasers, and they diffused too much to make a full ring of light on the structure. Thankfully, I found this cool effect of pointing lasers into the edge of acrylic mirrors. With the purple light, there was a fan that would turn on every time the laser was on, creating a nice motion of the purple light on the floor. To finish the piece, I programmed some simple patterns of flashing and fading for the purple light to follow.
