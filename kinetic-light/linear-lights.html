linear lights

in collaboration with cc and luna

video by me

this project was pretty fun to make! cc told me that she had iridescent acrylic squares, and i thought it would be cool to have a bunch of discrete objects refracting light, instead of one larger object. i made a lamp with one led's and a bunch of orbeez in a plastic cup that turned out really well one time freshman year, so i knew that this idea had potential.

there were some issues with figuring out the group, but it ended up fine in the end. there was also an issue with the addressable led's not working. whatever!!!!!!!!!! whatever dude!!!! it's fine, it ended up fine

originally, we were going to use led's below transluscent fabric to refract onto the ceiling, but we thought it wasn't bright enough, especially since the classroom wouldn't have perfectly dark conditions. so, we ended up making a box with the refractions showing on the walls of the box. because the light hits the surface closer, it looks brighter. however, i think the issue was actually that the light was hitting the iridescent acrylic from the bottom, isntead of from the top. i realized in retrospect that the refractions are actually from reflections, so to see them on the ceiling, the light should have been above the acrylic. i think this idea worked out though, since then the led would have been visible. i also really liked the idea of the peephole, but the piece does also look cool with overhead, school lighting. i think in the end, the peephole is more unique, so i'm happy with having made that decision.

making the circuit for the linear actuators was very easy. i think that we could have made the box taller so that the linear actuators could have been placed differently, since it was difficult to make the fabric move that much with how they were able to be set up. i think it's fine though, since even tiny movements create a lot of change in the refractions.

i was also happy with having painted the wood a darker color on the outside, but not painting the inside. i think it creates a sense of separation between the outside world and the inside world of the box and its silly lights and whatnot.
